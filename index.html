The Meerkats

Meerkats are small mammals that live in groups in the desert regions of southern Africa. They are known for their social and cooperative behavior, especially in protecting their group, where some individuals act as sentinels while others forage for food. They live in underground burrows, which help them stay safe from extreme heat and predators. Their diet consists of insects, small vertebrates, and plants. Meerkats have a well-defined social hierarchy and highly developed vocal communication.
